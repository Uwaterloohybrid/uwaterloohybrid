The Future is Electric

Fourth Year Capstone Project - In Hub Electric Powertrain

The goal for Waterloo Hybrid is to compete in the upcoming electric competitions of Formula Hybrid and Formula SAE Lincoln. Part of this effort is our fourth year capstone design project to design, test and build Waterloo Hybrid's first ever all electric powertrain.

The Motors

The heart of the powertrain are two custom electric motors, each independently driving the two rear wheels. Combined, the motors are capable of outputting over 60 HP in tractive power. The motors have been generously provided by Allied Motion.

In Hub Packaging

Instead of placing the motors inboard in chassis, the entire motor assembly is mounted directly onto the wheel assembly. This design is extremely compact and fits within the wheel itself. Not only does it contain the motors, it takes on the role of suspension upright. As a result, the overall system weight is reduced by eliminating the need for additional components.

Gear Reduction

An electric powertrain does not have to mean compromised performance. The motor assembly features a custom designed planetary gearbox reduction to make the most of the motors. Custom gears have been generously provided by Ontario Drive and Gear.

Torque Vectoring

Inherent to the design of the powertrain is the ability to individually control the torque of each wheel. Torque is continuously split between the two sides of the vehicle, augmenting the vehicle handling and vehicle stability.
